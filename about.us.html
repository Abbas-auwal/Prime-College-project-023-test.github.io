An eclipse is an event that occurs when one heavenly body such as a moon or planet moves into the shadow of another heavenly body. there are two types of eclipse, Solar and Lunar eclipse. Solar eclipses happen at new moon while Lunar eclipses happen at full moon. Solar eclipses are only visible from a narrow strip of land over which the Moon shadow passes while Lunar eclipses are visible from the whole nighttime hemisphere. Solar eclipse last from 10 seconds to about 7.5 minutes while Lunar eclipse lasts from about 30 minutes to over an hour.
